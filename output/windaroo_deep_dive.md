## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Windaroo Village is a small neighbourhood centre in Logan City's southern corridor. The centre has a **GP clinic (Qualitas Medical Practice) on-site** and a **permanently closed pharmacy (former "Windaroo Village Pharmacy" at Shop 17)** — meaning there's proven demand but also a failure history to understand. Two retail tenancies are currently available for lease (91 sqm and 198 sqm). The anchor tenant listed is **Foodworks** (not Woolworths/Aldi as initially suggested). The catchment population is small (~2,771) but affluent relative to broader Logan. Competition is moderate — the nearest pharmacies are 2-4km away. This is a **micro-catchment play** that lives or dies on the GP co-location and the centre's foot traffic.

## 9. FINANCIAL VIABILITY ESTIMATE (Back-of-Envelope)

### Revenue Assumptions
- Catchment: ~3,500 people
- Pharmacy utilisation rate: ~60% (general pop)
- Active customers: ~2,100
- Average scripts per customer per year: ~8-10
- Total scripts: ~17,000-21,000 pa
- Average script revenue (dispensing + markup): ~$18-22
- **Dispensary revenue: ~$306,000 - $462,000 pa**
- Front-of-shop (OTC, health/beauty): est. 30-40% of total
- **Estimated total revenue: ~$440,000 - $650,000 pa**

### Cost Assumptions
- Rent (198 sqm @ $350/sqm + outgoings): ~$85,000 pa
- Staff (pharmacist + 1-2 assistants): ~$200,000 - $280,000 pa
- COGS: ~55-60% of revenue
- **Break-even revenue: approximately $500,000-$550,000 pa**

### Verdict
The numbers are **tight**. At the lower end of revenue estimates, this pharmacy would struggle to break even. At the higher end (which requires capturing most of the catchment AND strong professional services revenue), it could produce a modest profit of $50,000-$100,000 pa.

This is NOT a high-volume opportunity. It's a **lifestyle/boutique pharmacy** play or a **satellite to an existing business**.

## 10. RECOMMENDATION

### Overall Assessment: ⚠️ PROCEED WITH CAUTION

**This is worth exploring further IF AND ONLY IF:**

1. **You can determine why the previous pharmacy closed.** Contact the former operator, the centre management, or the Pharmacy Guild. If it closed due to insufficient scripts/revenue, that's a red flag. If it closed due to operator personal issues (retirement, health, relocation), that's manageable.

2. **You can lock in a strong rent deal.** The lease should be well below $300/sqm net, with a 6-12 month rent-free fitout period and a fitout contribution from the landlord. The vacant tenancies and incentives suggest you have negotiating leverage.

3. **You can secure a referral relationship with Qualitas.** Meet with the practice manager. Understand their patient volumes, number of GPs, and whether they'd prefer an in-centre pharmacy. If they're scripting 100+ scripts/week that are currently being filled 3km away, you have a business case.

4. **You treat it as a satellite pharmacy or second site**, not a standalone play. The economics are too tight for this to be someone's only pharmacy.

### Immediate Next Steps
1. **Call McGrath (Ben Sands 0432 547 1xx)** — get actual rent, fitout contribution, lease terms
2. **Call Qualitas Medical Practice (07 3804 1700)** — enquire about GP volumes, interest in co-located pharmacy

### Bottom Line
The GP co-location is the compelling factor. Without it, don't bother. With it, plus a cheap lease and the right operator, this could be a modest but viable community pharmacy generating $500K-$650K revenue with thin but acceptable margins. It's a relationship-driven, service-oriented pharmacy — not a volume play.
